If you live on or near Spring Street — or anywhere in Redwood City that cares about safe residential streets — here is how to help, in rough order of impact.

## 2. Write to City Council

A short, personal email from a constituent has real weight. Three sentences is enough:

> *"I live on or near Spring Street between Bay Rd and 5th Ave. I support the Bay Road Complete Streets project, but I am asking the Council to fund equivalent calming on Spring Street so it does not become the cut-through route once Bay is calmed. The change is foreseeable — please plan for it now."*

Find your Council member at [redwoodcity.org/council](https://www.redwoodcity.org/departments/city-clerk/city-council).

## 3. Speak at a City Council or Transportation meeting

Public comment is logged. Three minutes from a resident is often what tips a project from "considered" to "funded." Watch the [city's meeting calendar](https://www.redwoodcity.org/) for upcoming Transportation Commission and Council meetings touching the Bay Road project.

## 4. Document what you see

If you witness dangerous driving on Spring Street, capture it:

- Phone video is enough. A few seconds, with the speed visible if possible.
- Tag posts with `#SpringStreetRWC` and the city's social handle (`@CityOfRedwoodCity`).
- Send footage to us — we will add it to the public [video archive on YouTube](https://youtube.com/@jazeee).

Documented incidents move the conversation from "we feel unsafe" to "here is what is happening, repeatedly."

## 5. Talk to your neighbors

Most of the people in our neighborhood agree that the street is dangerous. They just have not been organized. A single petition signature from each household has compounding political weight.
